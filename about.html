Vision Mission

We commit ourselves to Christ and His mission by providing relevant and responsive academic programs and active involvement in community service towards social transformation for the realization of the Reign of God.

Philosophy of Education

La Consolacion College Valenzuela is an Augustinian school operating under the influence of the Christian Spirit which aims to enflesh the Charism and Vision - Mission Statement of the Congregation which emphasizes a community spirit of friendship, compassion, responsibility, fairness, dynamism and love.



Institutional Goal


In order to live in our Mission and so attain our Vision, the school seeks to the attainment of these GOALS

    • To develop well-rounded person
    • To renew Christian faith and commitment
    • To build and live in community of friends
    • To inculcate socio-economic political awareness and preferential option for the poor


History

La consolacion College Valenzuela (formerly St. Jude Academy) proud of its scholastic achievements and community services is indebted of its existence to a golden-hearted man, Don Pastor Constantino of Polo, Valenzuela City. His generous gestures of donating the 3,000 square meters of land to the Augustinian Sisters of the Philippines (ASP) now Augustinian Sisters of Our Lady of Consolacion (ASOLC) in his newly developed Malinta Villages Subdivision in 1963, marked the beginning of a relevant institution that was to become the instrument of God to sow the seeds of living faith of the compassionate Christ in a community of friends.

The school’s humble beginning fell into the hands of Sr. Maria Pilar Prospero, OSA, the first religious Community Superior, together with Sr. Victoria Dormido, Sr. Matilde Cuevas, and Sr. Ma. Dolores dela Coronacion, the pioneers to undertake the education of the first thirty - five kindergarten pupils form the surrounding community.


On October 28, 1964 during the feast day of St. Jude Thaddeus, the papers of donation were turned - over to the ASP, and it was on the same year that the school acquired officially the name St. Jude Academy. Sr. Simpliciana de San Agustin, the first school principal worked on the school permit and subsequent recognition. As school being run by Religious, the parents were motivated to send their children to the first Catholic school in Valenzuela.


The school year 1966 to 1969, SJA experienced a change of administration under the leadership of Sr. Manuela del Redentor, OSA. The Private Schools Athletic Association (PRISAA) of Bulacan Catholic Schools recognized St. Jude Academy as an outstanding Catholic institution where athletics had a place in its curriculum. In the school year 1969-1970 another change of stewardship took place with Sr. Ma. Soledad Eamilao, OSA as Sister Superior-Principal.


More inputs in the field of academics became evident; the physical, moral and social spheres were also strengthened. In the same year, the organization of different student Assemblies, parent, and Alumni followed. For the school’s physical structures, the following were attained: the cementing of the entire school ground, the construction of the stage, the half south portion of the Sto. Niño building, and the improvement of the Science Laboratory and acquisition of equipment.


A re-shuffle of the school administrators took effect at the start of the school year 1978-1979 with Sr. Flor Cordis, OSA as the School Directress, and at the same time the Religious Coordinator, Sr. Mercedes Rivera, OSA as the High School principal and Sr. Eduarda de San Jose, OSA the Grade School principal. The process of development and expansion had continued since then, an indication of the Academy’s commitment to meet the growing needs of the student population and to further upgrade its academic standard.

In line with the Congregation acceptance of the Catholic School Systems Development (CS-SD), the teachers went through the Preparatory Orientation Talk on the program where improvements in the nine areas of school operation were given emphasis. Seminars about the program went on for almost three years. In 1981, the school came up with the basic education program (BED) whereby a gradual transition of only one school principal for both grade school and high school departments was adopted.


This was also the time when the school’s Board of Trustees (BOT) started to function. Sr. Mercedes Rivera, OSA took over the principalship. “Setting goal” had been the very words of Sr. Teresita Villaluz, OSA, the community coordinator in 1983. Through her motivation and creativity, together with Sr. Mercedes, the school underwent the First Congregational Evaluation Visit (CEV I). As a result, the Academy’s program had to focus more in the areas of Faculty, Instruction and Administration. In the year 1983-1984, Sr. Ermelinda Sales, OSA was assigned as the new principal. She managed the school for a year due to the fast turnover of the personnel in the Congregation.


In consonance with the achievement of relevant education and the articulation of the Charism and Mission Statement of the Congregation, a dynamic and seasoned academician – educator came by the school 1984-1985 in the person of Sr. Adelina Segismundo, OSA. A thorough review of the highlights and recommendations of the CEV I gave a new direction to the goals and objectives of the school. The following became more evident; Outreach Program through catechetical instruction in the nearby public school: jail visitation by students, teachers, and non-teaching personnel: rapport between the home and the school was strengthened; continuing growth and development of the teaching staff through seminar - workshops and attendance in the graduate school. Before the school year 1987-1988 started another sister - administrator came in, Sr. Natividad Varela, OSA as the new principal.


More promising projects were at hand in line with the institution’s celebration of its Silver Jubilee. At the disclose of the 90’s February 11, St. Jude Academy celebrated its 25th years of existence with the theme of “SJA’s 25th years of Service for Christian Value - Oriented Formation”. This was the same year when the school attained its program for building expansion due to the increase in the students and personnel population. In the school 1990 - 1991, another change of administration took place, Sr. Maria Annunciata Piella, OSA was assigned to be the principal. The ground–breaking of the north–wing portion of the main building (now Mother Rita building) ended her term of office.


In 1992-1993, through the given thrust of rendering a Filipino-Christian education and inspired by the Total Quality Management (TQM) of Sr. Francisca Montesa, OSA as the new principal, SJA gathered enough courage and determination to work for voluntary accreditation. On February 14 - 15, 1994, the school successfully passed the Congregational Evaluation Visit (CEV II). Because of the fast turn - over of personnel in the Congregation, school year 1995 - 1996 Sr. Rosalinda B. Bayla, OSA came in as the new school principal where the ground - breaking of Sto. Nino building started.

In school year 1996 - 1997, the immediate supervision of the school was entrusted to Sr. Celia Bayona, OSA who since her assignment in that year up to 1999 had proven the veracity of the Congregation’s appointment through the improvements undertaken in the physical plant. With her strong leadership, different physical renovations had been done: the completion and blessing of the new Sto. Nino building, the school chapel, comfort rooms, Instructional Media Center and the new Home Economics building. It is through her leadership too that the school held its PAASCU Preliminary Survey Visit in November, 1997. The institution received satisfactory results of the said visit and was given a period of one year to work on the accreditor’s recommendations.


In the school year 1999 - 2000, Sr Flor A. Cordis, OSA assumed again the responsibility of a school head of SJA. Sr Ma. Salvacion Taton, OSA as the community coordinator and school treasurer for two years and Mother Teresita Villaluz, OSA (the seventh Mother General of the ASOLC) assigned as OIC of two offices-Physical Plant and MRBCC, and at the same time as the school consultant. With their first term of office the high school department had its first PAASCU Formal Survey Visit on November 16-17, 1999, while the grade school department held its First Formal Survey Visit on November 21 - 22, 2001.


With that enthusiasm and commitment to service shown by the school constituents, the school passed the First Formal Survey Visit successfully. From that time on, both departments were granted the Certificate of Accreditation for three years making St. Jude Academy the first accredited school in the City of Valenzuela. The teamwork of the members of the Religious Community was the force behind that motivated everybody to work harmoniously for PAASCU accredited status of SJA. In 2001 - 2003, Mother Teresita became the community coordinator, after which, Sr. Flor A. Cordis, OSA took over the responsibility from 2003 - 2005.


School year 2003 - 2004, SJA celebrated its Ruby Jubilee with the theme: SJA: 40 years of Making the Difference through Innovative Education. It was a memorable festivity where simple yet educational and wholistic activities were planned initiated by the alumni. The academy has survived; we have managed to survive. SJA celebrated its invincibility against all odds . . . another triumphant year in addition to the many years of formation that it has provided for the students, to become value - oriented community leaders. Continuity of growth and development is in order. Attuned to the times of innovation, SJA’s gifts to the personnel and the clientele for the school year 2003 - 2005 were the internet and multi - media rooms and the speech laboratory, the next core of the system – communications and technology; Religion / Values Education being the first! To reminisce significant events, during the school year 2002-2003, a year before SJA’s Ruby Jubilee, the High School Department held its PAASCU Re - Survey Visit. It was a rewarding year as shown by the satisfactory results of the said visit ; the department was given a period of five years (from 2003 - 2008) to work on the recommendations.


School Year 2004 - 2005 was also a busy year for the Grade School Department due to the preparation for its first PAASCU Re - Survey Visit which was held January 19 - 20, 2005. Thus, gained rewarding results. The department was granted re -accreditation for a period of five years ( 2005-2010 ).


In school year 2005 - 2006, Sr. Evangeline B. Algaba, OSA, Director of the Association of Schools of the Augustinian Sisters (ASAS) and Religion Program, took over the principalship. While Sr. Ma. Linda Go, OSA as the school treasurer, and head of the administrative services whose coming back at SJA had proven strong partnership with the whole members of the personnel. The decisiveness, patience and sympathy of these two Religious, together with Sr. Brigida S. Baniquet, OSA , the religious community coordinator and teacher of Religion / Values Education subject, the school up - held continuously its vision – mission, rendered quality service to the larger community. To mention a few: the active participation of some third year and fourth year students in the annual Catechetical Seminar – Workshops at San Isidro Labrador Parish which had gained commendations from the parishioners, likewise, the Sunday Mass sponsor by the Judean Community. St. Jude Academy, had to take action and couldn’t be complacent, instead she had to fulfill the significant plans. It was in the same year that the launching of SJA’s 45th foundation anniversary was held with a challenging theme, Standard of Excellence Now and Onward…


This celebration was highlighted with the Search for Mr. and Ms. SJA 2007. The teamwork exerted by the SCB officers, the Personnel, and the Administration made the event worth – remembering. Furthermore in School Year 2009 – 2010, the school has to continuously make the difference, distinguished itself in the area of academic discipline, thus, enjoys the reputation brought about by accreditation of PAASCU. Likewise, the institution spearheads research projects prepared by subject area teachers and concerned offices.

As included in the Long – Range Development Plan of the school, the changing of its name from St. Jude Academy (SJA) to La Consolacion College Valenzuela (LCCV), where, by standard SJA is one of the ASOLC schools. Significant documents were submitted to Security Exchange Commission (SEC), thus, approved on February 2009. This move was an initial preparation for the opening of the tertiary level.


As it looks forward to the Golden Year of Consolinian-Augustinian education in the City of Valenzuela in 2013, LCCV strives to mold young minds who will help build a community spirit of friendship, compassion, responsibility, fairness, dynamism and love.
